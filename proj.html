Projects and Course Work

Optimal Design of eVTOLs for Urban Mobility using Analytical Target Cascading (ATC)

Tools:  MATLAB  |   Python 

Implemented a Multidisciplinary Optimization technique called Analytical target cascading on design of electric vertical take-off and landing aircrafts (eVTOLs). The project mainly involved modelling different subsystems such as motors, wings, propellers and batteries and then optimizing all the subsystems simultaneously while using individual optimization solvers.

Outcomes

  • Demonstrated convergence to optimum for a set of different ranges and payloads
  • Presented this work at Applied Energy Syposium which was organized by MIT and Harvard
  • Developed a generic ATC MATLAB package

Autonomous landing in the presence of heavy cross winds using nested MPC

Tools:  MATLAB  |   Simulink |   Flight gear

Implemented a nested MPC architecture in MATLAB Simulink to control and execute a cross wind landing of aircraft with variable wind gusts. This work was primarily done to test the effectiveness of nested MPC to respond to sudden disturbances. The results show that nested MPC performs well with satisfactory performance metrics for 200 iterations. Ofcourse, a realistic control algorithm should need more than a million iterations to comply with FAA standards but this work demonstrates a potential solution to an open problem till date.

Outcomes

  • Aircraft lands perfectly with even wind gusts upto 30knots
  • Recieved an A+ in this course
  • Validating using experimental data of motor performance

Design of Hybrid HydroKinetic Turbine

Tools :   ANSYS Fluent   |  AutoCAD

This was my submission for Bachelor's project at IIT Madras. This project focused on designing and testing a hybrid hydrokinetic turbine. This design innovatively combines the best of Savonius and Darreius turbines to get the best operating efficiency over a long range of RPMs. This design selectively operates Savonius turbine at low RPMs and Darrieus turbine at high RPMs using a spring-inertia based mechanism.

Outcomes

  • Developed a physical prototype to demonstrate the concept of hybrid turbine switching at a desired RPM
  • Achieved a higher efficiency by selectively enabling only the more efficient turbine at different operating RPM
  • Published a thesis and presented this work in BTP open house

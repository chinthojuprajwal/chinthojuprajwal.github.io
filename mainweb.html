If were to introduce myself I would start by saying that I'm a curious person. My interests have changed a lot in the past few years but my curiosity to learn new things has never changed.

I started off with an immense interest in learning about automobiles. Although I did get my bachelors from IIT Madras (2018), one of the most prestigious institutes in India, I was probably prouder when I was able to get a battery dead car running just from some concepts that I learned in my IC Engines class. I worked as an intern at Ashok Leyland (a HCV manufacturer in India), where I developed a porous heat exchanger simulator plugin module in ICEM CFD. In my final years of my undergrad program, i became interested in renewable energy, esppecially wind energy. Most of it was motivated by Prof. Dhiman Chatterjee's course on turbomachines. It is one thing to educate students and a completely different thing to motivate students ( more on my experience on mentoring here). I was fortunate to have him as my advisor for my bachelor's thesis on "Design of hybrid hydro-kinetic turbines".

I began a new chapter in life after I gradauated. I joined Wipro Automation as an Operations Executive in 2018. Before you make any assumptions, no, this is not the popular Indian IT giant corporation. Wipro automation started off as a new business unit in Wipro enterprises, a manufacturing arm of Wipro. When I joined, the entire business unit was a team of 5 people. I had no idea what an operations exectutive role meant at that time and I have no idea what it means now. I say that because I wore multiple hats in this organisation. My primary role was to lead asset management division where I built a portofolio of offerings, did business development and managed multiple partnerships. Outside of my division, I spearheaded the implementation of project management-based SAP workflow once our unit grew to almost 150 employees. I also looked after marketing aspects of the unit such as website development, social media presence and occasionally I used to prepare quartarly presentations for Mr. Azim Premji (the chairman of Wipro). Overall, it was a great experience to be involved in the growth of organization from a handful number of employees to a few hundreds now. I particularly value this experience because it thought me how to evolve into a succesful business unit while maintaining strict standards of a multinational corporation
